# 🕵️ Social Engineering Fundamentals

Social engineering is the art of manipulating people to gain confidential information or access.

## 🧠 Core Concept
Humans are often the weakest security layer, not systems.

Attackers exploit:
- Trust
- Authority
- Urgency
- Curiosity
- Fear

---

## 🎯 Objectives of Social Engineering
- Obtain credentials
- Gain unauthorized access
- Extract sensitive information
- Manipulate behavior

---

## 🧬 Psychological Principles

### 1. Authority
People tend to obey authority figures.

Example:
> “I'm from IT, I need your password to fix an issue.”

---

### 2. Urgency
Forces quick decisions without thinking.

Example:
> “Your account will be locked in 10 minutes!”

---

### 3. Social Proof
People follow what others are doing.

---

### 4. Reciprocity
If you give something, people feel obligated to return it.

---

### 5. Fear & Stress
Reduces critical thinking.

---

## ⚔️ Attack Surface

- Email (Phishing)
- Phone (Vishing)
- SMS (Smishing)
- Physical access
- Social media

---

## 🛡️ Defensive Mindset

- Verify identity ALWAYS
- Never share credentials
- Be skeptical of urgency
- Use MFA
- Report suspicious behavior

---

## ⚠️ Key Insight
Social engineering attacks do not hack systems — they hack humans.
