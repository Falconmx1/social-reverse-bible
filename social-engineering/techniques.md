# 💣 Social Engineering Techniques

This section covers real-world social engineering techniques used by attackers.

---

## 🎣 1. Phishing

Mass attack using fake emails to trick users into revealing sensitive information.

### 🎯 Objective
- Capture credentials
- Deliver malware
- Redirect to fake login pages

### ⚙️ Common Elements
- Fake domains (e.g., paypaI.com)
- Urgent language
- Malicious links or attachments

### 🧠 Example Scenario
> "Your account has been compromised. Click here to secure it."

---

## 🎯 2. Spear Phishing

Targeted phishing attack against a specific individual or organization.

### ⚙️ Characteristics
- Personalized information
- Appears highly legitimate
- Uses OSINT (social media, company data)

### 🧠 Example
> Email pretending to be the CEO requesting sensitive documents.

---

## 🎭 3. Pretexting

Creating a fake identity or scenario to obtain information.

### 🎯 Objective
Gain trust through a believable story.

### 🧠 Example
- Pretending to be IT support
- Acting as a bank representative

---

## 🪤 4. Baiting

Luring victims with something attractive.

### 🎯 Examples
- Free USB drives
- Free downloads (movies, software)

### ⚠️ Risk
Often delivers malware.

---

## 🚪 5. Tailgating (Piggybacking)

Gaining physical access by following authorized personnel.

### 🧠 Example
> “I forgot my badge, can you hold the door?”

---

## 📞 6. Vishing (Voice Phishing)

Attack via phone calls.

### 🎯 Objective
Extract sensitive info verbally.

### 🧠 Example
> “This is your bank, we detected suspicious activity.”

---

## 📩 7. Smishing (SMS Phishing)

Phishing via SMS messages.

### ⚠️ Example
> “Your package is delayed. Track it here: [malicious link]”

---

## 🧠 8. Impersonation

Pretending to be someone trusted.

### Targets
- Employees
- Executives
- IT staff

---

## 🕸️ 9. Watering Hole Attack

Compromising a website frequently visited by the target.

---

## 🧬 10. Quid Pro Quo

Offering something in exchange for information.

### 🧠 Example
> “I can fix your issue if you give me your login credentials.”

---

# 🛡️ Detection & Prevention

## 🔍 Indicators of Attack
- Urgent or threatening language
- Unexpected requests
- Suspicious links or attachments
- Requests for credentials

---

## 🧠 Defensive Strategies

- Security awareness training
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- Email filtering
- Verification protocols
- Zero trust mindset

---

## ⚠️ Final Insight

The most advanced attacks do not exploit systems — they exploit human behavior.
